General Knowledge (GK) Practice Test (100 Questions)

Test your knowledge across History, Geography, Science, and World Affairs.

Section 1: History & Culture (Q1 - Q25)

1. Who invented the theory of relativity?
Correct Answer: C (Albert Einstein)
2. Which historical event is known as the 'Great Fire of London'?
Correct Answer: B (1666)
3. The Renaissance movement began in which country?
Correct Answer: C (Italy)
4. Who was the first female Prime Minister of the United Kingdom?
Correct Answer: B (Margaret Thatcher)
5. The Great Wall of China was primarily built to protect against which group?
Correct Answer: B (Mongols)
6. Which year did the First World War begin?
Correct Answer: 1914
7. Who painted the Mona Lisa?
Correct Answer: Leonardo da Vinci
8. What is the ancient wonder, the Colossus, known for?
Correct Answer: A giant statue of the sun god Helios
9. Which civilization built the city of Machu Picchu?
Correct Answer: Inca
10. The Battle of Waterloo in 1815 marked the final defeat of which military leader?
Correct Answer: Napoleon Bonaparte
11. Who wrote the play 'Romeo and Juliet'?
Correct Answer: William Shakespeare
12. What was the capital of the Aztec Empire?
Correct Answer: Tenochtitlan
13. The Magna Carta was signed in which year?
Correct Answer: 1215
14. What is the famous phrase associated with Julius Caesar's crossing of the Rubicon?
Correct Answer: "Alea iacta est" (The die is cast)
15. The 'Silk Road' connected which two major regions?
Correct Answer: China and the Mediterranean/Europe
16. What material were the earliest coins made of?
Correct Answer: Electrum (a natural alloy of gold and silver)
17. In which year did the Berlin Wall fall?
Correct Answer: 1989
18. Who was the leader of the Soviet Union during the Cuban Missile Crisis?
Correct Answer: Nikita Khrushchev
19. Which famous ship sank in 1912 after hitting an iceberg?
Correct Answer: Titanic
20. What is the name of the oldest university in the world still operating?
Correct Answer: University of Bologna or Al-Qarawiyyin University
21. The period of the "Enlightenment" is also known as the "Age of ______"?
Correct Answer: Reason
22. Who was the Greek goddess of wisdom and warfare?
Correct Answer: Athena
23. The invention of the printing press is credited to whom?
Correct Answer: Johannes Gutenberg
24. What was the main trade item that fueled the conflict in the Opium Wars?
Correct Answer: Opium
25. Which famous Roman Emperor was assassinated on the Ides of March?
Correct Answer: Julius Caesar

Section 2: Geography & Environment (Q26 - Q50)

26. What is the largest desert in the world?
Correct Answer: Antarctica (non-polar: Sahara)
27. Which is the longest river in the world?
Correct Answer: Amazon River
28. The 'Ring of Fire' is a major area in the basin of which ocean?
Correct Answer: Pacific Ocean
29. What is the capital city of Canada?
Correct Answer: Ottawa
30. Mount Everest is located in which mountain range?
Correct Answer: The Himalayas
31. What is the name of the narrow strait that separates Europe from Africa?
Correct Answer: Strait of Gibraltar
32. Which country is geographically the largest in the world?
Correct Answer: Russia
33. What is the official language of Brazil?
Correct Answer: Portuguese
34. The Great Barrier Reef is off the coast of which country?
Correct Answer: Australia
35. Which continent is the least populated?
Correct Answer: Antarctica
36. What is the chemical formula for Ozone?
Correct Answer: $O_3$
37. Which major desert covers parts of Botswana, Namibia, and South Africa?
Correct Answer: Kalahari Desert
38. What is the lowest point on Earth's surface, located in the Pacific Ocean?
Correct Answer: Mariana Trench (Challenger Deep)
39. The international date line largely follows which meridian?
Correct Answer: $180^\circ$ meridian
40. What percentage of Earth's surface is covered by water?
Correct Answer: Approximately $71\%$
41. The currency of Japan is called what?
Correct Answer: Yen
42. What is the highest waterfall in the world?
Correct Answer: Angel Falls
43. What is the study of earthquakes called?
Correct Answer: Seismology
44. Which celestial body is sometimes called Earth's "sister planet"?
Correct Answer: Venus
45. What is the largest lake in Africa by surface area?
Correct Answer: Lake Victoria
46. The Suez Canal connects the Mediterranean Sea with which other sea?
Correct Answer: Red Sea
47. What is the term for a wind that changes direction with the seasons?
Correct Answer: Monsoon
48. Which country is separated from India by the Palk Strait?
Correct Answer: Sri Lanka
49. What is the official name of the disease commonly known as COVID-19?
Correct Answer: Coronavirus disease 2019
50. What geological process is responsible for the formation of mountains like the Alps and Himalayas?
Correct Answer: Plate Tectonics (Continental Collision)

60. What force keeps the Moon in orbit around the Earth?

72. What is the chemical element with the symbol Fe?

73. The phenomenon of light bending as it passes from one medium to another is called what?

74. What is a byte composed of?
